  <h1 class="sh-page-hero__title">Finance Terms</h1>
  <p class="sh-page-hero__lede">
    Plain-language definitions of the terms that come up again and again in
    valuation, M&amp;A, and investment banking. Written from the perspective of
    someone actually modeling and analyzing deals — not a textbook.
  </p>
</div>

## A

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Accretion / Dilution <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">A/D</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Whether a merger <em>increases</em> (accretive) or <em>decreases</em> (dilutive) the
      acquirer's pro forma EPS. Rough quick check: in a cash deal, accretive if target's
      net income yield exceeds the after-tax cost of cash; in a stock deal, accretive if
      the acquirer's P/E is higher than the target's <em>effective</em> P/E.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Alpha <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">α</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Excess return relative to a risk-adjusted benchmark. Positive alpha means a manager
      delivered more return than what their beta exposure would've predicted. Rarely
      persists — which is the point of passive investing.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">APV <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Adjusted Present Value</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      A DCF variant where you value the business as if all-equity financed, then separately
      add the PV of financing side effects (mainly interest tax shields). Useful when capital
      structure changes meaningfully over time — e.g., LBOs.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Arbitrage</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      In theory: riskless profit from simultaneous offsetting trades. In practice: almost
      always involves some residual risk (model, liquidity, financing). "Arb funds" play
      merger arb, convertible arb, statistical arb — each with its own risks.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## B

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Basis Point <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">bps</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      1/100th of a percentage point. 50 bps = 0.50%. Fixed-income and rates people live
      in bps; it's how you talk about anything sub-1%.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Beta <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">β</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Measures a stock's sensitivity to the market. β=1.0 moves with the market; β=1.5
      amplifies moves by 50%. Used in CAPM for cost of equity. For private companies,
      you "unlever" peer betas to strip out capital-structure effects, then re-lever to
      your target's structure.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Book Value</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Shareholders' equity on the balance sheet — what the accountants say the equity
      is worth. Usually diverges a lot from market value, especially for asset-light
      businesses where most value is intangible (brand, IP, human capital).
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Break-Up Fee</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      A penalty paid if a merger doesn't close for specified reasons. "Seller break fee"
      protects the buyer if the target walks (typical 2–4% of deal value). "Reverse
      termination fee" protects the seller if the buyer backs out.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Bullet Maturity</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      A bond or loan where the full principal is repaid at the end in one lump sum
      (no amortization). Most high-yield bonds and Term Loan B facilities in LBOs are bullets.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## C

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">CAPM <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Capital Asset Pricing Model</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      <em>R<sub>e</sub> = R<sub>f</sub> + β × (R<sub>m</sub> − R<sub>f</sub>)</em>.
      The standard way to estimate cost of equity. Flawed but universal — everyone critiques
      it, everyone still uses it.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Capital Structure</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The mix of debt and equity financing a company. More debt lowers WACC (up to a point)
      via tax shields, but raises distress risk. The optimal structure balances these —
      different industries sit at very different equilibria.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">CapEx <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Capital Expenditures</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Cash spent on long-lived assets (PP&amp;E). Split into <em>maintenance</em> (keeps the
      business running) and <em>growth</em> (expands capacity). For DCF, what matters is
      the total — both reduce FCF.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Carry <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Carried Interest</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The GP's share of profits above a hurdle rate in a PE/VC fund — usually 20%. It's why
      PE partners get rich: returns on personal capital aren't large, but carry on $1B+ of
      LP capital compounds fast.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Cash Conversion Cycle <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">CCC</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      DSO + DIO − DPO. How many days of working capital are tied up in the operating cycle.
      Lower is better — negative CCC (Amazon, Costco) means suppliers are financing your
      growth.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Comparable Companies <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Comps</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Valuation by applying peer multiples to your target's metrics. The easiest method
      to do, the hardest to do <em>well</em> — peer selection drives everything.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Convertible Bond</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      A bond that can be converted into equity at a pre-set ratio. Priced like a straight
      bond + a call option on equity. Popular with growth companies that want cheaper
      coupons and don't mind potential dilution.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Covenant</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Contractual restrictions in a credit agreement. <em>Maintenance covenants</em> are
      tested periodically (e.g., Net Debt/EBITDA &lt; 5.0x); <em>incurrence covenants</em>
      only bind when the company takes a specific action (e.g., issues more debt).
      "Cov-lite" loans skip most maintenance tests.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## D

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">DCF <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Discounted Cash Flow</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Intrinsic value method. Forecast unlevered FCF, discount by WACC, add a terminal
      value. Garbage-in-garbage-out: small changes in WACC or terminal growth move the
      answer wildly, which is both its power and its danger.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Deferred Tax</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Timing differences between book and tax accounting. Deferred tax <em>liabilities</em>
      typically come from accelerated tax depreciation; <em>assets</em> often come from
      NOL carryforwards. Matters in M&amp;A because DTA/DTL survive the transaction
      (subject to §382 limits).
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Dilution</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Reduction in ownership percentage from newly issued shares (options, SBC, secondary
      offerings, acquisition currency). Always model <em>fully diluted</em> share count using
      the treasury stock method for options/warrants.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Dividend Discount Model <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">DDM</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Value of equity = sum of PV of future dividends. Most useful for mature, stable
      dividend payers (utilities, banks). The Gordon Growth form: <em>P = D<sub>1</sub> /
      (r − g)</em>.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">DSO / DIO / DPO</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Days Sales Outstanding, Days Inventory Outstanding, Days Payables Outstanding.
      The three components of the cash conversion cycle — each measures how fast that
      working capital bucket turns over.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">DuPont Analysis</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Decomposes ROE into three drivers: Net Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier.
      Lets you see whether a company's ROE comes from profitability, efficiency, or leverage.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## E

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">EBITDA</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, D&amp;A. A proxy for operating cash flow that's
      neutral to capital structure and depreciation policy — which is why it's the
      denominator in most enterprise-level multiples. Charlie Munger famously hated it
      ("bullshit earnings").
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">EBIT <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Operating Income</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Earnings before interest and taxes. Closer to "real" earnings than EBITDA because
      it respects D&amp;A. Use EBIT when comparing capital-heavy businesses where D&amp;A
      is a meaningful economic cost.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Enterprise Value <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">EV</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Market cap + debt + preferred + minority interest − cash. The value of the company
      to <em>all</em> capital providers. Use EV multiples when comparing companies with
      different capital structures — equity multiples will distort the comparison.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">EPS <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Earnings Per Share</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Net income / diluted share count. The single number Wall Street fixates on. Always
      reconcile <em>GAAP EPS</em> vs. <em>adjusted EPS</em> — the bridges (SBC, restructuring,
      amortization of intangibles) tell you how aggressive management is with adjustments.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">ERP <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Equity Risk Premium</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The extra return investors demand for holding equities over risk-free bonds. Historical
      US ERP is ~5–6%; forward-looking estimates (Damodaran's implied ERP) are typically
      lower. Small changes here cascade through every DCF.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Exit Multiple</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The multiple applied to terminal-year EBITDA in a DCF (or LBO) to get terminal value.
      Should equal roughly what you'd pay for a comparable mature company — not your own
      entry multiple projected forward.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## F

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">FCFF / FCFE</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      <em>Free Cash Flow to Firm</em> (unlevered) goes to all capital providers; discount
      by WACC. <em>Free Cash Flow to Equity</em> (levered) is what's left for equity after
      debt service; discount by cost of equity. Most valuation work uses FCFF.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Fairness Opinion</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      A letter from an investment bank stating that the consideration in a deal is "fair
      from a financial point of view." Protects the board from shareholder lawsuits.
      Almost never says a deal is <em>unfair</em> — the bank selection process sees to that.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Float</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Shares available for public trading (excludes insider-held, restricted, and treasury
      shares). Low-float stocks are more volatile and easier to manipulate. Also a term in
      insurance: the premiums held before claims are paid (Buffett's engine).
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## G

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Goodwill</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The premium paid in an acquisition over the fair value of identifiable net assets.
      Sits on the balance sheet; not amortized (GAAP) but tested annually for impairment.
      Big goodwill write-downs = management admitting they overpaid.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Gordon Growth</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Perpetuity formula used for DCF terminal value: <em>TV = FCF<sub>n+1</sub> / (r − g)</em>.
      Only valid when r &gt; g. Keep g at/below long-run nominal GDP (~2–4%).
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Greenshoe</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      In an IPO, an over-allotment option letting underwriters sell up to 15% more shares
      than planned if demand is strong. Named after the Green Shoe Manufacturing Co., the
      first IPO to use it.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## H

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Haircut</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      A discount applied to the market value of an asset when used as collateral. 5% haircut
      on $100 of securities = $95 of borrowing capacity. Haircuts widen in stressed markets —
      a key amplifier in financial crises.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Hurdle Rate</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The minimum return an investment must clear to be worth pursuing. For projects, it's
      usually WACC (or WACC + spread). For PE funds, a hurdle above which GP carry kicks
      in (typically 8%).
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## I

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Impairment</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      A write-down of an asset's carrying value when its recoverable amount drops below
      book value. Common for goodwill, intangibles, and long-lived assets. Hits the income
      statement but is non-cash.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">IPO <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Initial Public Offering</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The first sale of a private company's shares to the public. Involves roadshows, an S-1,
      bookbuilding, and a lock-up period (usually 180 days) before insiders can sell.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Intrinsic Value</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      What a business is "really worth" — usually the PV of its future cash flows. Compare
      to market value to find mispricings. Graham's core concept: price is a vote, intrinsic
      value is the business.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">IRR <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Internal Rate of Return</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The discount rate that makes NPV = 0. Expressed as an annualized %. PE funds target
      ~20%+ IRRs. Be careful: IRR ignores scale (small fast returns can beat large slow ones),
      and it assumes reinvestment at the IRR itself.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## L

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">LBO <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Leveraged Buyout</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Acquisition financed mostly with debt — typically 60–80% of the purchase price.
      Returns come from (1) debt paydown, (2) EBITDA growth, and (3) multiple expansion.
      The LBO model is the single most common test in PE interviews.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Leverage</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Use of borrowed money to amplify returns. "Turns of leverage" = multiples of EBITDA
      (e.g., 5.5x levered means Net Debt = 5.5 × EBITDA). Amplifies both gains and losses.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Liquidity Ratios</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Measures of short-term solvency: Current Ratio (CA/CL), Quick Ratio ((Cash+AR)/CL),
      Cash Ratio (Cash/CL). Rule of thumb: Current &gt; 1.5x is healthy; Quick &gt; 1.0x
      is cushioning against inventory risk.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## M

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Margin of Safety</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The discount between your estimate of intrinsic value and the price you'll pay.
      Graham: you don't need to be certain — you need a buffer against being wrong.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">MoIC <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Multiple on Invested Capital</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Total proceeds / total equity invested. A 3.0x MoIC over 5 years ≈ 25% IRR. PE funds
      report both — MoIC tells you <em>how much</em> you made, IRR tells you <em>how fast</em>.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Mezzanine</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Debt that sits between senior debt and equity in the capital stack. Higher yield,
      often with warrants or equity kickers. Used to fill the "funding gap" in LBOs.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## N

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">NAV <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Net Asset Value</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Total assets minus total liabilities — effectively book equity. The standard valuation
      for REITs, investment funds, and holding companies. Public REITs often trade at
      premiums or discounts to NAV depending on sentiment.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Net Debt</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Total debt − cash &amp; equivalents. The logic: excess cash could theoretically pay
      down debt, so economically what matters is the net figure. Some analysts subtract only
      "excess" cash (above what's needed for operations).
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">NOPAT <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Net Operating Profit After Tax</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      EBIT × (1 − T). Starting point for FCFF. Strips out the tax benefit of interest so the
      cash flow reflects the business's operating economics, not its financing choices.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">NPV <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Net Present Value</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Sum of discounted cash flows minus initial investment. Positive NPV = value-creating.
      Preferred over IRR when comparing mutually exclusive projects of different sizes.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">NWC <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Net Working Capital</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Operating current assets − operating current liabilities. Excludes cash and debt
      because those are financing items. Changes in NWC are a cash-flow item in the DCF.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## O

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Operating Leverage</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The sensitivity of operating income to revenue changes, driven by fixed-cost intensity.
      High operating leverage = big EBIT swings from small revenue moves (software, airlines).
      Low = more stable (distribution, services).
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## P

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">P/E Ratio</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Share price / EPS. The most-quoted valuation ratio. Affected by capital structure,
      accounting choices, and growth — which is why it's best used with companies that
      share all three.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">PIK <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Paid-In-Kind</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Interest that accrues to principal instead of being paid in cash. Common in mezz
      debt and stressed balance sheets. Good for cash flow in the short run, terrible for
      leverage over time.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Precedent Transactions</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Valuation based on multiples paid in past M&amp;A deals for similar targets. Usually
      gives <em>higher</em> multiples than trading comps because they include control
      premia and synergies.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Pro Forma</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Financial statements adjusted "as if" a transaction had already occurred. In M&amp;A,
      pro forma EPS combines acquirer and target, plus synergies, minus financing costs —
      the input for the A/D test.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">PPA <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Purchase Price Allocation</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Post-close, the buyer allocates the purchase price across tangible assets, identifiable
      intangibles (customer relationships, tech), and goodwill. Creates new D&amp;A from
      the stepped-up basis — a key model input.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## Q

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Quality of Earnings <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">QoE</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Diligence report that normalizes reported EBITDA to "real" recurring EBITDA — backs
      out one-time items, aggressive accounting, and owner add-backs. The number that
      actually gets lended against in an LBO.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Quick Ratio</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      (Cash + ST Investments + AR) / Current Liabilities. Like the current ratio but strips
      out inventory — assumes you can't quickly turn inventory into cash at book value.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## R

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Risk-Free Rate <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">R<sub>f</sub></span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The theoretical return on a riskless asset — in practice, the yield on a government
      bond matched to your investment horizon. US DCFs typically use the 10Y UST.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">ROE <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Return on Equity</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Net Income / Avg. Equity. How efficiently equity capital generates profit. Can be
      juiced with leverage — always decompose with DuPont before praising high ROE.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">ROIC <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Return on Invested Capital</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      NOPAT / Invested Capital. Arguably the single best operating metric: it measures
      returns on <em>all</em> capital (debt + equity) and isn't distorted by leverage.
      Value is created when ROIC &gt; WACC.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Revolver <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Revolving Credit</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      A line of credit that can be drawn, repaid, and redrawn up to a committed limit.
      Usually cheaper than term debt, with an unused commitment fee. In LBO models, the
      revolver is the "plug" that keeps cash from going negative.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## S

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Sensitivity Analysis</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Running the model with different inputs to see how outputs move. Usually presented
      as a 2D grid (WACC × terminal growth → implied share price). Shows where your answer
      is fragile.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Short Selling</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Borrowing shares, selling them, and hoping to buy back lower to return to the lender.
      Max gain is capped (price → 0), max loss is unlimited (price → ∞). Shorts pay borrow
      fees and dividends on the borrowed stock.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">SPAC <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Special Purpose Acquisition Co.</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      A shell company that IPOs with cash, then looks for a private company to merge with
      (a "de-SPAC"). A backdoor path to public markets. Had a huge 2020–2021 bubble that
      largely ended in pain for retail investors.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Stub Period</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The fractional year at the start of a DCF when you're valuing mid-year. E.g., valuing
      at 6/30 with a 12/31 fiscal year gives a 0.5-year stub. Mid-year convention handles
      this cleanly by discounting each year by (t − 0.5).
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Synergies</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Extra value from combining two companies. <em>Cost synergies</em> (eliminating duplicate
      functions, procurement scale) are usually credible. <em>Revenue synergies</em>
      (cross-sell, new markets) almost always disappoint.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## T

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Tax Shield</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The value of deductions that reduce taxable income. Interest tax shield =
      Debt × Interest Rate × Tax Rate. A major reason debt is "cheaper" than equity in WACC.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Tender Offer</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      A direct offer to a target's shareholders to buy their shares at a specified price.
      Used as a takeover tactic (especially hostile) to go around the target's board.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Terminal Value <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">TV</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The lump-sum value representing all cash flows beyond the explicit forecast period.
      Typically 60–80% of a DCF's total value — which is why DCF is so sensitive to long-term
      assumptions.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Treasury Stock Method <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">TSM</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The way diluted share counts treat options and warrants: assume in-the-money options
      are exercised, and the proceeds are used to buy back shares at the current price.
      Net new shares = options − buyback.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## U

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Unlevered Beta</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      A peer's beta stripped of the impact of its capital structure. Let you isolate the
      pure <em>business</em> risk, then re-lever to your target's structure. Formula:
      <em>β<sub>U</sub> = β<sub>L</sub> / (1 + (1 − T)(D/E))</em>.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Underwriter</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The bank (or syndicate) that manages a securities issuance. In IPOs, they price the
      deal, allocate shares, and provide after-market support. In bond deals, they often
      commit capital to buy and resell.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## V

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">VIX</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      CBOE's "fear index" — the market's 30-day implied volatility on S&amp;P 500 options.
      Normal ~15; stressed &gt;25; crisis &gt;40. VIX spikes mean options prices are pricing
      in a lot of uncertainty.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Volatility</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Standard deviation of returns. <em>Historical</em> volatility = realized from past
      prices. <em>Implied</em> volatility = derived from current option prices. Divergence
      between the two is what options traders hunt for.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## W

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">WACC <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Weighted Avg. Cost of Capital</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The blended after-tax cost of all capital: <em>(E/V) × R<sub>e</sub> + (D/V) × R<sub>d</sub> × (1 − T)</em>.
      The discount rate for unlevered DCFs. Weights should use market values, not book.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Waterfall</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The rules defining how proceeds from a sale or liquidation flow to different
      stakeholders. In PE, "European waterfall" = carry paid after LPs are fully repaid;
      "American waterfall" = carry paid deal-by-deal (GP-friendly).
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## Y

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Yield Curve</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The plot of yields across maturities. Normal = upward-sloping (long rates &gt; short).
      <em>Inverted</em> (short &gt; long) has historically preceded US recessions. Flatness
      signals uncertainty about future growth.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">YTM <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Yield to Maturity</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The IRR of a bond if held to maturity — the rate that equates PV of future coupons
      and principal to current price. "The yield" people quote on bonds.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## Z

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Zero-Coupon Bond</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      A bond with no coupon payments — issued at a discount to face value, pays full face
      at maturity. Price = FV / (1 + YTM)<sup>n</sup>. T-bills are the classic example.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Z-Score <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Altman</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      A formula combining five ratios (working capital, retained earnings, EBIT, equity
      market value, sales — all scaled by total assets) to flag bankruptcy risk. Z &gt; 3 =
      safe; &lt; 1.8 = distress zone.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>
