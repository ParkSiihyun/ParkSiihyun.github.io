  <p class="sh-page-hero__lede">
    Plain-language definitions of the terms that come up again and again in
    valuation, M&amp;A, and investment banking. Updated as I encounter new ones.
  </p>

## A

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Accretion / Dilution <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">A/D</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Whether an acquisition <em>increases</em> (accretive) or <em>decreases</em> (dilutive) the
      acquirer's pro forma EPS. Tested by comparing the acquirer's P/E to the target's effective
      P/E (inverse of after-tax cost of financing).
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Alpha <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">α</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Excess return relative to a benchmark, after adjusting for risk (beta). Positive alpha
      means a manager outperformed the market-implied return given the risk taken.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## B

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Beta <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">β</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      A measure of a security's volatility relative to the overall market. Beta of 1.0 =
      moves with the market; &gt;1.0 = more volatile; &lt;1.0 = less volatile. Used in CAPM to
      derive the cost of equity.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## C

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">CAPM <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Capital Asset Pricing Model</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      A model for the required return on equity:
      <em>R<sub>e</sub> = R<sub>f</sub> + β(R<sub>m</sub> − R<sub>f</sub>)</em>.
      The most common way to estimate cost of equity in a DCF.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Comparable Companies <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Comps</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      A valuation method that values a company based on multiples (EV/EBITDA, P/E, etc.)
      of similar public peers. The "easy" half of every IB pitchbook.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## D

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">DCF <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Discounted Cash Flow</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Intrinsic valuation method. Forecast unlevered free cash flows, discount by WACC,
      add a terminal value (Gordon growth or exit multiple). The gold standard — and also
      the method most sensitive to inputs.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## E

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">EBITDA</h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation &amp; Amortization. A proxy for operating
      cash flow that's independent of capital structure — which is why it's the denominator
      in the most-used enterprise value multiple.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">Enterprise Value <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">EV</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Market cap + debt + preferred + minority interest − cash. The total value of a company
      to all capital providers. Use EV (not equity) multiples when comparing companies with
      different capital structures.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## L

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">LBO <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Leveraged Buyout</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      Acquisition of a company using a significant amount of borrowed money. Returns are
      driven by (1) debt paydown, (2) EBITDA growth, and (3) multiple expansion.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>

## W

<div class="sh-glossary">

  <div class="sh-glossary__item">
    <h3 class="sh-glossary__term">WACC <span class="sh-glossary__abbr">Weighted Avg. Cost of Capital</span></h3>
    <p class="sh-glossary__def">
      The blended after-tax cost of a firm's equity and debt, weighted by their market
      values. The discount rate used in unlevered DCF.
    </p>
  </div>

</div>
